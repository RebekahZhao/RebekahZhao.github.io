Research

I am interested in developing energy-efficient integrated circuits for wireless communication and Biomedical Implants. My research projects include:

  • Imaging with 60GHz microwave

    Advisor: Jeyanandh Paramesh, Aswin Sankaranarayanan
    Summer 2017 - Present

    The goal of this project is to use s-parameter measurements of 60GHz millimeter waves reflected by objects to characterize high-resolution surface feature. An antenna is placed on both the transmission and receiving side. A motion stage moves the transmitter antenna on a 2D plane to scan target object with certain resolution. The transmitter sends waves of multiple frequencies, and phase information of S11 and S21 of each frequency is extracted and analyzed to achieve absolute distance of each scanning position. [Slides]

  • 3D reconstruction of outdoor scenes from polarization cues and photometric stereo

    Advisor: Aswin Sankaranarayanan
    Fall 2016 - Present

    Due to the linear nature of sun movement, traditional photometric stereo of outdoor scenes require a long data acquisition time to generate reliable results. This method combines the method of polarization with photometric stereo. Not only does it significantly reduce the data collection time, it also bypasses the azimuth ambiguous issue with polarization based scene reconstruction. [Slides]

  • Ultra high-density EEG system design

    Advisor: Pulkit Grover, Shawn Kelly, Jeff Weldon
    Fall 2015 - Spring 2016

    I have been involved in this project for designing high-density EEG measurement systems. My mission was to study novel wet and dry electrode designs suitable for very dense EEG caps. I have also worked on testing a spherical harmonics based amodel to validate that more information can be acquired from a high-density measurement system.
